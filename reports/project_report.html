01 · Objective

Project Objective

Build a complete, production-ready machine learning system that enables businesses to understand customer behaviour, predict risk, and take data-driven retention actions.

Problem Statement
Businesses lose significant revenue to preventable customer churn. Without predictive intelligence, retention efforts are reactive and inefficient — spending budget on customers who were never at risk or missing customers who were about to leave.
Solution
Combine churn probability and lifetime value predictions into a two-dimensional customer intelligence system. Segment customers by risk and value. Simulate retention ROI. Deliver predictions through a live REST API and interactive dashboard.
Business Impact
A total retention campaign investment of $66,780 recovers an estimated $4.70M in revenue — a net ROI of 7,043% — by prioritising the right customers with the right intervention.

🔍 Key Engineering Decision

Recency was kept in the dataset for use in the Composite Risk Score during segmentation, but strictly excluded from both model feature sets. This separation ensures no leakage while retaining its value as a behavioral signal for the downstream segmentation pipeline.

✅ Recall = 1.0000 — All 214 Churners Caught

The threshold of 0.30 was selected through F-beta optimisation with beta=2, which weights recall twice as heavily as precision. In a retention context, missing a churning customer (false negative) is far more costly than incorrectly flagging a retained customer (false positive). The result: zero missed churners at test time.

⚠️ Note on Notebook vs Script Metrics

Notebook CV F1 of 0.8406 reflects exploratory analysis with evolving feature sets. Script F1 of 0.8770 is the final reproducible benchmark with fixed train/test split (random_state=42) and confirmed feature set. Script metrics are the production standard.

Feature 1 — Avg_Order_Value
Dominates with SHAP 1.0000 — 10× larger than the second feature. Hard threshold at ~$50: above = retain (SHAP +8 to +11), below = churn (SHAP -2). Single most powerful churn signal.

Below 5 adds → churn signal. Above 5 → retain signal. Wishlist activity reflects product interest breadth and future purchase intent. Appears in top 3 of both models.
⚠️ Single Feature Dominance — Known Limitation

Avg_Order_Value is 10× more important than the second feature in the churn model. This creates a blind spot: high-spending customers receive near-zero churn probability regardless of other behavioral signals. This limitation was confirmed by the stress test and directly motivated the composite risk score approach in segmentation.

Churn is driven by spending level and friction. LTV is driven by behavioral volume and habit. 11 · Customer Segmentation

Customer Value Segmentation

A two-dimensional segmentation framework combining predicted LTV and composite risk score assigns all 10,000 customers to one of six behavioural segments — directly actionable by retention and marketing teams.

⚠️ Methodological Challenge — Solved

Initial segmentation using churn probability threshold (0.30) left At-Risk VIP and Vulnerable segments empty — all 1,404 high-risk customers had Low LTV. SHAP analysis confirmed this was the Avg_Order_Value blind spot. Solution: Composite Risk Score (40% churn + 30% recency + 30% cart abandonment) combined with within-tier percentile ranking — matching production approaches used at Amazon, Netflix, and Spotify.
